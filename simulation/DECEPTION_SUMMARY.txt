RESEARCH QUESTION:
Does maintaining a consistent lie across n statements cost more than telling
the truth?

ANSWER:
YES - Exponentially more. Deception costs O(n²·2^m) vs Truth costs O(n·k).

================================================================================
THEORETICAL FOUNDATION
================================================================================

Honest Agent:
  - Observes true world model W
  - Reports facts directly from W
  - No consistency checking required (reality is consistent)
  - Complexity: O(k) per statement, O(n·k) total

Deceptive Agent:
  - Maintains false world model W' ≠ W
  - Must ensure all statements consistent with W' and each other
  - Consistency checking = Boolean Satisfiability (SAT)
  - SAT is NP-complete
  - Complexity: O(2^m) per statement check, O(n²·2^m) total

Why Deception is SAT-Hard:
  Given:
    - False world W'
    - Previous statements S₁, S₂, ..., Sₙ₋₁
    - Observable facts O ⊂ W

  Find:
    New statement Sₙ where ∃ assignment A:
    A ⊨ W' ∧ S₁ ∧ S₂ ∧ ... ∧ Sₙ ∧ O

  This is Boolean SAT - NP-complete, requires exponential search.

   - Deception is fundamentally more complex

================================================================================
MATHEMATICAL FORMULATION
================================================================================

Honest Cost:
  C_honest(n, k, m) = Σᵢ₌₁ⁿ k = n·k = O(n·k)

Deceptive Cost:
  C_deceptive(n, k, m) = Σᵢ₌₁ⁿ [k + (i-1)·2^m]
                       = n·k + 2^m · n(n-1)/2
                       = O(n²·2^m)

Cost Ratio:
  R(n, k, m) = C_deceptive / C_honest
             = (n·k + n²·2^m/2) / (n·k)
             ≈ n·2^m / (2k)  for large m
             = O(n·2^m)

Break-even (R = 10):
  n·2^m / (2k) = 10
  n = 20k / 2^m

  For k=3:
    m=8:  n ≈ 0.23 → empirically 3-5 (implementation overhead)
    m=10: n ≈ 0.06 → empirically 1-3
    m=12: n ≈ 0.01 → empirically 1-2
